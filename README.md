## Project Background
This project focuses on analyzing a survey of data professionals from LinkedIn, containing over 600 participants. The goal is to extract meaningful insights from those working in the data industry and identify important trends. Therefore, serving as a valuable resource for individuals considering a career in the data field.

## Executive Summary
The analysis is based on a dataset of 630 survey participants, providing insights into various data professions. This information was visualized in a Power BI dashboard and revealed key insights in average salary, preferred programming language, difficulty of breaking into the field, and overall happiness. The analysis shows that most of the survey participants are from the United States where those in the data field also had the highest average happiness score based on salary and work/life balance, compared to those in Canada, India, United Kingdom, and other countries.

## Insights Deep-Dive

### Satisfaction (Happiness) Score Analysis

- Of the 630 people, the average age of participants in this survey is 30 years old.
  - 41% of participants are from the United States, 12% are from India, 6% are from the United Kingdom, 5% from Canada, and 36% listed their country as “Other”.
- Participants in the data field reported a moderate level of satisfaction with their work/life balance, reflected in an overall average happiness score of 5.74 out of 10.
- With an overall average of 4.27 out of 10, the salary happiness scores of the participants suggest significant dissatisfaction among data professionals regarding their compensation in relation to their current job positions.
- When focusing on the majority of those that took the survey, participants from the United States gave the highest scores of happiness with their work/life balance with a 6.38 out of 10. The same trend also shows with the happiness score of their salaries at a 5 out of 10.


### Salary Analysis

- The role of Data Scientist has the highest overall average yearly salary at 94k, followed by these data roles:
  - Data engineer: 65k
  - Data Architect: 64k
  - “Other” Data Role: 60k
  - Data Analyst: 55k
- In Canada and the United Kingdom, “Other” data roles have the highest yearly salary at 120k and 61k, respectively. 
- Salaries for data professionals are lowest in India and countries categorized as "Other." This trend typically reflects a combination of economic, social, and policy influences.
- Data professionals employed in the United States earn substantially more than the overall average, with their salaries being, on average, 40% higher.
  - Data Scientist: 139k (48% higher)
  - Data Engineer: 101k (55% higher)
  - Data Architect: 76k (19% higher)
  - “Other” Data Role: 83k (38% higher)
  - Data Analyst: 79k (44% higher)
 

### Difficulty Entering the Data Field
- Overall, 42.7% of survey participants had an “Average” degree of difficulty breaking into the Data field.
- 21.27% of those who took the survey had an “Easy” degree of difficulty entering the Data field.
- Approximately a quarter of the survey participants (24.76%) found it "Difficult" to become a Data professional.
